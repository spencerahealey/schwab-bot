# Investment Strategy

## Philosophy

This portfolio is **thesis-driven, not target-allocation driven**. No fixed percentage targets. Positions size up or down based on conviction and market conditions, not arbitrary rebalancing.

**Three questions govern every position:**
1. Is the original thesis still intact?
2. Has the risk/reward changed?
3. Are there better opportunities for this capital?

**If a thesis breaks, exit — even if the position is down. If a thesis strengthens, add — even if it's already the largest holding.**

## Account Structure

_Customize this section for your account types. Common setups:_

- **Tax-advantaged (Roth IRA, 401k)**: Higher-volatility, high-upside plays. Tax-free growth means bigger swings are acceptable.
- **Taxable brokerage**: Conviction holds optimized for long-term capital gains. Sells trigger taxes — factor that into every exit decision.

## Standing Rules

- No sacred cows — every position gets scrutinized regardless of past performance
- Concentration is fine when conviction is high, but always quantify the downside
- Don't over-trade — most months the right answer is "hold"
- Always note tax implications on taxable account sells
- Surface new ideas — if research finds a compelling name, add it to the watchlist
- Never rebalance for the sake of rebalancing
